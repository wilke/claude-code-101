# 2026-05-04 — Normalizing the treatment_group labels

Spent the session getting `treatment_group` down to a clean set. The raw column
has the same three groups spelled many ways: `control`, `Control`, `CTRL`, and
`" control "` (with stray spaces) all mean *control*; `low`/`Low`/`LOW` and
`high`/`High`/`HIGH` likewise. A naive `value_counts()` shows **six** groups,
which makes every per-group mean wrong.

- **Decision.** Normalize with `str.strip().str.lower()`, then map the one
  non-obvious alias `ctrl → control`. After that the set is exactly
  `{control, low, high}`. Put an `assert` on that set so a new bad label fails
  loudly instead of silently becoming a fourth group.
- **What I tried first (dead end).** My first alias map had `"ctrl": "high"` — a
  copy-paste slip. The clue was that the `high` group suddenly had ~100 rows
  instead of ~64, and `control` was too small. Caught it by comparing group
  counts before/after the map. Lesson: always print group counts after a
  relabel, never trust the map blindly.
- **Parameter.** Canonical group set: `{control, low, high}`. Expected rough
  counts control ≈ 200, low ≈ 120, high ≈ 80 (proportions ~50/30/20).

**Open question:** are the stray spaces in `" control "` from the instrument
export or from a manual edit? If the export is the source, other columns may have
the same whitespace problem and should be stripped too.
