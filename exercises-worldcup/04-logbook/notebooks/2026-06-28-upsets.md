# 2026-06-28 — K=40 overshoots the early-round upsets

Now that the group stage and round of 32 have been played, scored the Elo predictor
against actual results — and the K=40 choice from 06-20 looks too hot.

- **Observation.** The early rounds had the usual crop of upsets (a couple of lower-rated
  sides beating favourites). With **K=40**, a single upset swung the winner's rating by
  a huge margin — enough that the model then *over*-rated that team going into the next
  round and mispredicted its follow-up match. The ratings were chasing noise: one
  surprise result was being treated as a big, permanent change in team strength.
- **Test.** Re-ran the same matches with **K=20** and **K=24**. Lower K absorbed the
  upsets as the variance they mostly are, the follow-up predictions improved, and the
  held-out log-loss over the R32 was **better** than with K=40.
- **Tension with 06-20.** The 06-20 note picked **K=40 because it's the FIFA/community
  "standard"**. This directly conflicts: standard-for-the-official-ranking is optimizing
  a different thing (a stable published ranking) than we are (per-match predictive
  accuracy through a short, upset-heavy tournament). For *our* objective, K=40 overshoots.
- **Leaning.** Drop to **K≈24** for the predictor, or — better — make K depend on match
  importance / stage rather than a single global value. Not fully resolved.

**Open question:** should `K` be **adaptive** — larger early when ratings are uncertain,
smaller later, or scaled by how surprising a result was — instead of one constant? That
would reconcile "responsive to real form" with "not fooled by one upset". Needs a proper
sweep against held-out matches before committing.
